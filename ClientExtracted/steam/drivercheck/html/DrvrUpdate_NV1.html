Note: Video driver updates may request updates to more recent Windows Service Packs.

In the unlikely event video driver installation fails, follow instructions at the driver support page. You may have to revert to an earlier video driver version.

In addition, there is a slight chance upgrading video drivers can cause problems with other graphics-intensive applications (e.g. X, Y, Z).
If this occurs, the other application may have updates or patches available on their website for download.
If the other graphics application is critical and updates are unavailable, you may need to revert to older video driver.
Visit the driver support page for details on reverting to older video drivers.
Any problems with other graphics applications from updating video drivers should be reported to the application vendor and the video card manufacturer.
